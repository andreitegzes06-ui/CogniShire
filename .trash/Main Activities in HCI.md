### The Artisan’s Workshops
#### Main Activities in Human-Computer Interaction

> [!note] Entering the Workshops
>
> HCI is not only a design field and not only a technical field. It works by combining ideas from Computer Science, Cognitive Science, Psychology, Design, and empirical research.


---

### The Three Workshop Rooms

> [!abstract] Room I — The Scribe’s Study
> ![[assets/photos/theory_room.jpg|500]]
>
> **Academic focus:** Theory in HCI
>
> This room explains how HCI uses concepts such as **mental models**, **cognitive load**, **attention**, **perception**, **affordances**, and **feedback** to understand how users interact with systems.
>
> Theory helps explain why some interfaces feel natural and why others feel confusing.
>
> [[02_Activities/Theory in HCI|Enter the Scribe’s Study]]

> [!info] Room II — The Observation field
> ![[assets/photos/experiment_room.jpg|500]]
>
> **Academic focus:** Experiment in HCI
>
> This room explains how HCI studies real users through **usability testing**, **A/B testing**, **interviews**, **surveys**, **eye-tracking**, task completion time, error rates, and user satisfaction.
>
> Experiment helps researchers check whether an interface actually works for humans, not only whether it works technically.
>
> [[02_Activities/Experiment in HCI|Enter the Observation Chamber]]

> [!example] Room III — The Interface Forge
> ![[assets/photos/design_room.jpg|500]]
>
> **Academic focus:** Design in HCI
>
> This room explains how HCI creates and improves interfaces through **wireframes**, **prototypes**, **interaction flows**, **visual hierarchy**, **accessibility**, and user feedback.
>
> Design turns theory and experimental results into usable systems.
>
> [[02_Activities/Design in HCI|Enter the Interface Forge]]
